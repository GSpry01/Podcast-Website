Podcast Genres and Where to Start

Podcasting genres are as wide and varied as movie genres.

Genres and Their Great Shows

Comedy
Comedy Bang Bang- Host Scott Auckerman interviews comedians and celebrities, and also an assortment of characters. You will find your next favorite comedian from this podcast.
My Brother, My Brother, and Me- Three Brothers host an advice show, full of advice you shouldn't take.
Off Book- Two improv comedians improvise a new musical every week with their guest.
Audiodrama
Limetown- Investigative Journalist Lia Haddock sets out to discover what happened to the residents of Limetown and why they dissapeared 10 years ago.
The Black Tapes- Jounalist Alex Reagen profiles famed paranormal debunker Dr. Richard Strand, but comes across a cabinet full of black tapes. These tapes cannot be debunked by Dr.Strand.
Wolf 359- Doug Eiffel is the communications officer for the U.S.S. Hephaestus Research Station, a space station that orbits the star Wolf 359, 7.8 light years from Earth. This show is one part space adventure, one part character drama, and mix in one part absurdist sitcom.
Documentary/ Investigative Journalism
S-Town- Follows a man named John who despises his Alabama town and decides to do something about it.
Serial- An investigative podcasts, Serial unfolds one story - a true story - over the course of a whole season.
Fantasy/ Role Playing
Critical Role- A group of professional voice actors play Dungeon and Dragons. (Now an animated show on Amazon Prime)
The Adventure Zone- The family from "My Brother, My Brother, an Me" play Dungeons and Dragons with their dad.
Dungeons and Daddies- A hilarious podcast where, a group of dads get transported to a fantsy realm where the must rescue their sons and try to return home.
Finance
Planet Money NPR show that teaches finance in an easy way through stories so you can undertstand the basic concepts
How To Money- Ways to save and grow your money, while still being asble to spend on things that bring you joy.
